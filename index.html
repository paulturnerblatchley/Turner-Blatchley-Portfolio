02 Creative Approach

This reel highlights the creative storytelling techniques used to transform standard event coverage into an emotionally engaging narrative that showcases student achievement and community partnership.

Narrative Interweaving

By intercutting interviews from opposing team members, I created a compelling storyline that built tension and showcased the competitive spirit of the event.

Personal Transformation Story

Featured a particularly compelling student narrative about how the mock trial program transformed him from a shy student into a confident, well-spoken senior.

Authentic Student Voice

Prioritized student perspectives rather than authority figures, letting the participants share their own experiences and passions directly with the audience.

Emotional Pacing

Carefully structured the narrative arc to build emotional investment, using music and editing rhythm to enhance the competitive tension and personal stories.

03 Technical Challenges

Challenging Lighting Conditions

The courtroom wasn't designed for video production, creating suboptimal lighting conditions that required creative solutions during filming and post-production.

Solution:

Applied advanced color grading techniques to enhance contrast, balance exposure, and create a consistent look across all footage, making lighting issues virtually unnoticeable in the final product.

Noisy Audio Environment

Student interviews were captured outside amidst a noisy crowd of participants, resulting in audio that was initially considered potentially unusable by the team.

Solution:

Employed advanced audio restoration techniques, including noise reduction, EQ adjustments, and subtle compression to isolate voices while preserving natural sound quality, making the background noise virtually unnoticeable.

Time-Sensitive Narrative Construction

The competition was ongoing during filming, requiring rapid identification of compelling storylines and efficient editing to capture the event's energy while it was still current.

Solution:

Implemented a streamlined editing workflow with quick-turnaround techniques, including pre-built templates and efficient media organization, allowing for rapid narrative construction while maintaining high production quality.

Case Study #4

Golden Bell Awards

Showcasing educational excellence through compelling video storytelling that celebrates innovative programs and exceptional educators.

Client Fontana Unified School District
Project Summer Bridge Program
Role Producer, Editor, Graphic Designer

01 Project Overview

The Golden Bell Awards recognize outstanding educational programs across California. As part of SBCSS's celebration of county winners, our team produced video profiles showcasing each program. This case study highlights the Fontana Unified School District's Summer Bridge Program video, one of eight videos produced for the awards ceremony.

Multi-Video Project

Part of a coordinated effort to produce eight distinct videos for different school districts, requiring consistent quality and branding while highlighting each program's unique aspects.

Student Mentorship Focus

Showcased how the Summer Bridge Program creates a culture of peer mentorship, with older students helping younger ones transition to high school, creating a powerful positive feedback loop.

Dynamic Visual Approach

Created an innovative map-based motion graphics intro that transitioned to on-the-road footage, establishing a unique visual identity that broke from conventional approaches used in previous years.
Fontana Unified School District's award-winning Summer Bridge Program

02 Creative Approach

Journey-Based Narrative

Created a compelling "journey" narrative by developing a custom map-based motion graphics sequence that transitioned to on-the-road footage, symbolizing the physical and metaphorical journey to achieve educational excellence.

Sustainability Focus

Highlighted each program's unique strength in creating a cyclical culture, carefully selecting interview segments that demonstrated this positive feedback loop and showing visual evidence of sustainability in action.

Visual Storytelling

Employed a dual-camera setup to capture authentic student interactions, strategically scheduling filming during active program hours to document genuine mentorship moments that substantiated the interview narratives.

Cohesive Presentation

Mixed and normalized all 8 videos for aural consistency. Customized motion graphics for the intro, lower thirds and transitions to maintain visual cohesion, creating a recognizable series while honoring each program's individual identity.

03 Production Process

Pre-Production Planning

Coordinated with the senior communications specialist to develop a production schedule for all eight videos within a tight timeline. For the Fontana video, researched the Summer Bridge Program and prepared tailored interview questions to elicit compelling responses about peer mentorship.

Team Coordination Collaborated with senior communications specialist and digital media specialist
Research Program background, key stakeholders, and visual opportunities
Planning Production schedule, equipment list, and interview questions

On-Location Production

Traveled to each school to film their program in action. Set up dynamic lighting and dual-camera configurations to conduct in-depth interviews with students, teachers, and administrators while capturing authentic B-roll of program activities interactions.

Post-Production

Developed the custom map-based motion graphics intro and lower thirds in After Effects. Selected and arranged the most impactful interview segments to highlight the most impactful narrative. Performed comprehensive audio mixing to ensure consistency across all eight videos in the series.

Video Editing

Crafted a narrative-driven edit that balanced stakeholder perspectives with authentic student experiences

Motion Graphics

Created custom animated lower thirds and transitions that established visual continuity across all eight videos

Audio Engineering

Performed detailed audio mixing to ensure clarity and consistency throughout the production

04 Challenges & Solutions

Tight Production Timeline

With eight videos to produce for different districts while maintaining consistent quality, the timeline presented significant challenges for planning, production, and post-production.

Solution:

Developed a systematic production workflow with the communications team that divided responsibilities while maintaining quality control checkpoints. Created reusable motion graphics templates that could be quickly customized for each video.

Diverse Program Environments

Each Golden Bell program operated in different environments with varying visual opportunities and constraints, making it difficult to maintain visual consistency across all videos.

Solution:

Adapted lighting and camera setups for each unique school environment while maintaining consistent interview framing. Created a flexible shooting strategy that could accommodate both indoor classroom settings and outdoor activities.

Balancing Multiple Narratives

The Summer Bridge Program involved various stakeholders with different perspectives on the program's value, making it challenging to create a focused narrative while honoring all voices.

Solution:

Identified the recurring theme of peer mentorship across interviews and made this the central narrative thread. Selectively edited interviews to create a cohesive story while ensuring all key stakeholders' perspectives were represented.
